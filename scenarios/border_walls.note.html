Border walls: shaping paths at the seam between players

Each player defends a wedge with its own rotated grid. Two build options let a player shape how demons move through their wedge and across the seam where the two coordinate systems meet. The blue lines are demon path trails.

PERP rung (perpendicular to the border) — a tangential wall spanning the wedge at one ring, with a gap left at one border. Stacking rungs with the gap alternating border-to-border forces demons to zig-zag: they can only get closer to the center when they reach a border and drop through its gap. Watch a single demon snake left–right–left down player 0's wedge.

ALONG wall (along the border) — a segment lying on the seam that seals crossing at that ring. Here player 0 seals its whole seam with player 1 except the center-most ring, so after snaking down, demons must exit at the center. Pathfinding reroutes automatically — sealed crossings are dropped from the flow field.

Because a crossing joins cells at the same radius on both sides, two adjacent players can align rungs at the same ring and a demon transitions from one wedge to the next without jumping radius — player 1 has matching rungs, so the snake continues after the crossing.
